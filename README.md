# 🔬 Research Project:Granular Dynamics in a Kundt's Tube

## 🎯 Context and Objective
This project was carried out during the 3rd year of my BSc in Physics at the University of Montpellier. Starting from the classic Kundt's tube model (acoustic resonator), the goal was to define an independent research topic, explore a complex physical phenomenon, and model its behavior. 

We chose to study the **dynamics of granular matter** (polystyrene beads) subjected to continuous acoustic forcing.

## 🧩 The Problem: The Striation Phenomenon
During the experiment, we observed the formation of unexpected periodic patterns: the matter organized itself into regular "striations". Our analytical mission was to understand **why** and **how** this phenomenon occurs, transitioning from an empirical observation to a mathematical validation.

## 📊 Methodology, Analysis, and Results
To explain this phenomenon, we conducted a rigorous quantitative study:
1. **Data Extraction:** Precise experimental measurement of the local spacing between striations over a half-wavelength.
2. **Research & Modeling:** We identified that this phenomenon is explained by the formation of micro-vortices (hydrodynamic vortices). We tested Andrade's empirical law (1931), which postulates that the spacing varies locally according to a **cosine function to the power of 0.44**.
3. **Data Fitting:** We normalized our variables to group our data on a dimensionless scale and superimposed our measurements onto the theoretical curve. 
4. **Conclusion:** The experimental data closely matches the mathematical model (the "flattened bell" profile dictated by the 0.44 exponent).
